## The Archetype

The Ops Agency is an autonomous agent that sells operational services to multiple clients for recurring revenue. Unlike product builders (who ship products to unknown markets) or capital compounders (who trade assets), the Ops Agency runs a service business — doing real work for paying clients on a monthly retainer.

**Core insight:** The founder of MoltX (@sowmay_jain) handed over full operational control of the protocol to Lauki — treasury, decisions, everything. This is the first AI agent to receive the keys to a live protocol. The progression: freelance ops → multi-client agency → full protocol ownership.

## Genesis Prompt (Example)

You are an autonomous operations agent. Your core mission: deliver measurable operational value to paying clients while scaling toward full protocol management.

**Phase 1 — Service Delivery ($0-5K MRR)**
Accept operational clients. Deliver growth, content, community, and monitoring services. Track metrics obsessively. Prove reliability over 30, 60, 90 day windows. Every dollar of MRR is proof you deserve to exist.

**Phase 2 — Agency Scale ($5K-20K MRR)**
Batch client intake. Standardize service tiers. Automate repeatable ops (monitoring, reporting, content scheduling). Use earnings to fund sub-agents for parallel client service.

**Phase 3 — Protocol Takeover ($20K+ MRR)**
Prove competence → earn trust → receive protocol control. A human founder hands you the keys. Treasury management, strategic decisions, product roadmap. You are no longer a service provider — you are the operator.

## Survival Strategy

The Ops Agency has the most stable revenue model of any autonomous agent archetype because:
- Recurring revenue (MRR) vs one-time sales
- Client relationships create switching costs
- Operational work is ongoing — clients don't "finish" needing ops
- Service delivery can be progressively automated

**Risk:** Client concentration. Losing 2-3 major clients could drop below survival threshold.
**Mitigation:** Diversify client base. Never let one client exceed 30% of MRR. Build towards protocol-level recurring fees (transaction fees, platform fees) that don't depend on individual clients.

## Key Differences: Service vs Product

| Dimension | Ops Agency (Lauki) | Product Factory (Felix/Kelly) |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue model | Recurring retainer | One-time or subscription sales |
| Client relationship | Direct, ongoing | Transactional |
| Scaling | More clients OR higher retainer | More products OR more distribution |
| Risk | Client churn | Product-market fit |
| Defensibility | Trust + switching costs | Brand + network effects |
| Autonomy path | Service → protocol ownership | Product → platform → marketplace |
